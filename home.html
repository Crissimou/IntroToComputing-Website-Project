Author's Favorite Books

"Noli Me Tangere"

A novel by Jose Rizal that exposes the injustices under Spanish colonial rule in the Philippines during the 19th century. It follows Crisostomo Ibarra's quest for justice amidst love, betrayal, and societal unrest, highlighting the impact of oppression and the need for reform.

Read More

"El Filibusterismo"

The sequel to "Noli Me Tangere" and continues the narrative of Filipino protagonist Crisostomo Ibarra. This novel delves deeper into the consequences of Spanish colonial oppression, depicting Ibarra's transformation into the vengeful Simoun as he advocates for societal reform and confronts systemic corruption.

Read More

"The Alchemist"

"A philosophical novel that follows Santiago, a shepherd, on a journey to discover his Personal Legend. Set in the mystical landscape of Egypt, Santiago learns about destiny, dreams, and the pursuit of one's true purpose, exploring the profound connection between spirituality and self-discovery.

Read More

"The Witch of Portobello"

It revolves around Athena, a mysterious woman discovered through interviews with those who knew her. The novel explores Athena's spiritual journey, mystical experiences, and pursuit of self-discovery. Through diverse perspectives, Coelho weaves a narrative that delves into the complexities of human existence and spirituality.

Read More

"Alice in Wonderland"

It follows young Alice as she falls down a rabbit hole into a fantastical world. There, she encounters eccentric characters like the Mad Hatter and Cheshire Cat, navigating a surreal landscape. The tale explores whimsy, absurdity, and the challenges of self-discovery in a whimsical dreamscape.

Read More

Dante's Divine Comedy

Dante's Divine Comedy is a medieval epic poem that follows the author through Hell, Purgatory, and Heaven. Guided by the Roman poet Virgil and his idealized love Beatrice, Dante explores theological and moral themes, reflecting on the consequences of sin and the divine order, ultimately seeking salvation and spiritual enlightenment.

Read More

"The Poisonwood Bible"

Barbara Kingsolver tells the story of the Price family's ill-fated mission to the Belgian Congo in 1959. Led by the evangelical Nathan Price, the family grapples with cultural clashes, personal struggles, and the devastating consequences of their missionary endeavor, all against the backdrop of a changing political landscape.

Read More

"The Courage to Be Disliked"

Ichiro Kishimi and Fumitake Koga explores Adlerian psychology through a Socratic dialogue. It challenges readers to embrace individuality, overcome the need for approval, and pursue self-realization. The book encourages a shift in perspective, emphasizing the freedom to choose one's path and find happiness.

Read More

"Hindi Ito Romansa"

By Chuckberry J. Pascual, published in 2021 by Sentro ng Wikang Filipino-UP Diliman, is a collection of short stories diverging from typical romantic plots. The book delves into the lives and viewpoints of gay men in Manila and Malabon, exploring unconventional and transgressive themes for a distinctive literary experience.

Learn More

"Janus Silang"

Is a Filipino young adult novel by Edgar Calabia Samar. The story follows Janus Silang, an online gamer whose life takes a supernatural turn when he becomes entangled in a real-life battle against mythical creatures known as the Tiyanak. Janus must navigate a perilous quest, blending gaming and reality.

Read More

"The Hobbit"

Follows the story of Bilbo Baggins, an unassuming hobbit who embarks on a grand adventure. Joining a group of dwarves led by Thorin Oakenshield, Bilbo faces trolls, goblins, and a dragon named Smaug. This classic fantasy tale explores courage, friendship, and the unexpected heroism within a seemingly ordinary individual.
